LITERATURE REFERENCES FOR VALIDATION

RESIST MATERIALS:

[1] Oyama, K., et al. (2016)
    'Estimation of resist sensitivity for extreme ultraviolet
     lithography using an electron beam'
    AIP Advances 6, 085210
    → PMMA sensitivity: ~500 mJ/cm² (Our simulation: 500 mJ/cm²)

[2] Mohammad, M.A., et al. (2012)
    'Study of Development Processes for ZEP-520'
    Japanese Journal of Applied Physics 51, 06FC05
    → ZEP520A sensitivity: 80 mJ/cm², LER: 2-8 nm
    → Our results: 80 mJ/cm², LER: 2.7 nm ✓

[3] Gorelick, S., et al. (2011)
    'High-efficiency Fresnel zone plates for hard X-rays'
    J. Synchrotron Radiation 18, 442-446
    → PMMA for X-ray lithography, aspect ratios >10:1


X-RAY LITHOGRAPHY FUNDAMENTALS:

[4] Cerrina, F. & White, D. (2000)
    'X-ray Lithography'
    Materials Today, Vol. 3, Issue 10
    → Optimal energy: 0.5-2 keV for sub-micron features
    → Beer-Lambert law for absorption

[5] Khan, M. & Cerrina, F. (1989)
    'Modeling proximity printing in X-ray lithography'
    J. Vac. Sci. Technol. B 7, 1430
    → Fresnel diffraction for proximity effects


MEMBRANE MATERIALS:

[6] Holmes, W., et al. (1998)
    'Measurements of thermal transport in low stress
     silicon nitride films'
    Applied Physics Letters 72, 2250-2252
    → Si₃N₄ thermal conductivity: ~20 W/m·K
    → Our model: 20 W/m·K ✓

[7] Vila, M., et al. (2003)
    'Mechanical properties of sputtered silicon nitride'
    J. Appl. Phys. 94, 7868
    → Young's modulus: 100-210 GPa (depends on stoichiometry)
    → Our model: 250 GPa (stoichiometric Si₃N₄)

[8] Sekimoto, M., et al. (1982)
    'Silicon nitride single-layer X-ray mask'
    J. Vac. Sci. Technol. 21, 1017
    → Silicon-rich nitride for reduced stress


X-RAY MASK TECHNOLOGY:

[9] Vladimirsky, Y., et al. (1999)
    'X-ray mask technology and applications'
    Microelectronic Engineering 46, 365-372
    → Ta, W, Au absorbers compared

[10] Gorelick, S., et al. (2010)
     'Direct e-beam writing of high aspect ratio nanostructures'
     Microelectronic Engineering 87, 1052-1056
     → PMMA for X-ray optics, aspect ratio >11:1
